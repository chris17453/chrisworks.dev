About Me

I started as a developer and architect building enterprise systems across multiple industries - accounting platforms, inventory management, e-commerce integrations, shipping systems with FedEx and UPS, payment processing, you name it. Early on, I built a loyal client base ranging from boutique shops to enterprise operations, and I've kept many of them running and evolving for years - migrating them to cloud, rebuilding legacy systems, whatever they needed.

Mid-career, I decided to try corporate life. Started at Pitney Bowes as a Senior Engineer where I built and integrated hundreds of shipping platform integrations in C#. I was both leading the technical direction and writing the code. They closed my division exactly one year after hiring me. Moved to E*TRADE as a Principal, building infrastructure and integrating systems - platform scanning, VM automation, VMware integration. This was during COVID, so I was also gearing up the C-suite with reports and metrics on what they needed for a remote workforce. Then Morgan Stanley bought them. Went to IBM as a Senior Architect where I traveled the country working with banks and top-tier corporate divisions, focusing on OpenShift, Turbonomics, Instana, and WatsonX. I wasn't just designing solutions - I was implementing them. Then they gutted the 401k and restructured compensation.

After IBM, I went back to running my own practice. Spent a year mixing vacation with interesting projects for local and remote clients. Now I'm ready for the next lead role - bringing both the scrappy adaptability of a consultant and the process understanding of someone who's navigated multiple Fortune 500 environments. I know how to ship, how to scale, and how to deal with the reality of enterprise technology. I lead from the front - I can architect it, build it, and fix it myself.
